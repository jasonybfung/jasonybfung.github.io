TEACHING

Practicing the piano can be a highly rewarding activity.

In diagnosing and resolving problems of technique, musicality and personal interpretation, students build qualities of focused attention, dilligence and problem-solving; they also benefit from a solo activity that can provide them with the much-needed time to self-reflect and decompress from their everyday lives.

As a teacher, I believe in being attentive and addressing each of my students' needs on an individual basis. Through offering a pragmatic approach to the piano technique, students will gain the independence to continue their progress outside of lesson, as well as the freedom to pursue their own goals, musically or otherwise.
