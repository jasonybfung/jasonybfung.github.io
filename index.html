BIO

Canadian-Hong Kong pianist Jason Fung is a student of Jimmy Brière at Université de Montréal. Prior to completing a Master of Music (2018) with David Jalbert at the University of Ottawa, Jason studied at Queen's University, where he graduated from a B.Mus with distinction and was winner of the Queen's University Concerto-Aria Competition in 2016.

As a teacher, Jason's pedagogical interest and approach centers on building ergonomic patterns of movement at the piano. Drawing from a lifelong background as an athlete, as well as an interest in human anatomy brought on by a shoulder injury in 2015, it is his goal with provide every student a complete means of comfort at the piano - thus freeing them up to express themselves well.

During his time in Canada , Jason has also benefitted from additional work with world-class faculty, including pianists Angela Park, Stéphane Lemelin, and Maneli Pirzadeh. He has also played in masterclass with Angela Hewitt and Jerome Lowenthal. Inquire about lessons here or read articles on piano playing and teaching on his blog - here.
